As Artémidore, the player embodies a small crab in the search of his disappeared friend and parental figure : a gigantic Hermit Crab. This journey leads him inside the empty shell of his friend where he discovers a confusing oniric world representing his travel through loss and self reconstruction.


This adventure game has the particularity to feature a world without up or down, therefore the player can only move by walking on surfaces, regardless of it's orientation. They can only leave a surface for another by diving in a water current and grabbing the next surface.


Artémidores other skills can be unlocked and combined by the player in ordre to choose their favorite playstyle and thus represent the self reconstruction of the protagonist. Examples of skills are the possibility of grabbing seaweed to hide its shell from predators or having an extensible pincer allowing to grab things further. Combining those skills grants a better access to camouflage.

My work

Player controller : Our 3rd person controller allows the player to walk on any surface regardless of common gravity. Therefore, I practiced a lot of 3D Mathematics to orient and move the character smoothly according to the surface's normal (averaged from several raycasts) and the camera's orientation.


Pathfinding : Our swimming enemy needed to move in 3 dimensions, using Unity's NavMesh was therefore not an option. I chose to implement the A* algorithm and adapted what i learned in a tutotrial to our needs by expending it to the thrid dimension.
Adding a whole dimension was of course way more demanding in ressources. Thanks to the heap optimization showed in the tutorial it was sufficient for our demo (featuring a rather small level) but would not be functionnal in a final product.


Finite State Machine : I also worked on our enemy AI and learned how to build a Finite State Machine with ScriptableObjects, this method had the advantage of creating multiple states by combining actions and transitions. The AI uses the A* algorithm for navigation.
