Overly Attached Machina

ID

Nov. 2017
School Project

Duration : 3 days
Tools : Makey Makey, Unity, JoyToKey

Roles

Fabrication
Game/UX Design

Overly Attached Machina is an installation composed of a box representing the inside of an AI. There are buttons on two opposite sides of the machine and two players have to press the correct buttons in order to satisfy the AI. However, the must not touch each other or the AI will get jealous. The buttons being behind the back of each player, they have to get really close but without touching, and communicate to choose the buttons they press.


For this project we used arcade buttons to check the players' inputs and a Makey Makey using conductivity to check wether the players are in contact or not. Those informations are then processed in Unity and players recieve feedbacks through speakers.


What I learned

This project was incredibly interesting, the installation allowed us to design an experience involving the player's body in a physical space we created. I learned how to build an experience with new possibilities out of the classical screen/gamepad scheme. The fabrication of the installation was also technicaly enriching as I could experiment with new materials such as the Makey Makey Arduino card, arcade buttons and, of course, a lot of wire.
