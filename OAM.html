Overly Attached Machina

Info

Nov. 2017
School Project

Duration : 2 weeks
Tools : Makey Makey, Unity, JoyToKey

Roles

• Fabrication
• UX/Game Design

Overly Attached Machina is an installation composed of a box representing the inside of an AI. There are buttons on two opposite sides of the machine and two players have to press the correct buttons in order to satisfy the AI. However, they must not touch each other or the AI will get jealous. The buttons being behind the back of each player, they have to get really close but without touching, and communicate to choose the buttons they press.


For this project we used arcade buttons to check the players' input and a Makey Makey using conductivity to check whether the players are in contact or not. This information is then processed in Unity and players recieve feedback through speakers.


My work

I took part indesigning our exeprience relying on the players' body and how they interact with the space and each other. I also handled the hardware setup, working with a Makey Makey Arduino card, arcade buttons and, of course, a lot of wire.
